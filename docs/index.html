Overview

LITORA (Lighting-grounded Image/Text Open Relighting Assets) is the first large-scale, openly-available portrait relighting dataset. It cross-mixes 70K FFHQ portraits with 2,698 real-world CC0 HDRI environment maps from Poly Haven and ambientCG, providing over two orders of magnitude more subject diversity than any existing open portrait relighting dataset.

Construction Pipeline

The AnyLight pipeline cross-mixes FFHQ portraits with HDRI environments through four automated stages.

  1. HDRI Processing — Extracts SH lighting, dominant light detection, and LDR background viewports via perspective sampling with ACES tone mapping.
  2. HDRI-Conditioned Relighting — IC-Light (SD v1.5) foreground-background compositing with classifier-free guidance and two-phase denoising.
  3. QA-Gate — Six-dimensional quality scoring (identity, quality, alignment, face, boundary, lighting) with composite threshold τ=0.55 and retry mechanism. 98.6% first-attempt pass rate.
  4. VLM Annotation — Qwen2.5-VL generates per-sample transformation instructions bridging image-conditioned and text-conditioned training.

Sample Anatomy

Each sample: source portrait Is, foreground mask M, HDRI background B, relit target It, and VLM instruction T.

Every sample includes a source portrait, foreground mask, HDRI-derived background viewport, diffusion-relit target, VLM-generated transformation instruction, per-dimension quality scores, and generation provenance. This enables both image-conditioned (Is, M, B → It) and text-conditioned (Is, T → It) training.

Quality Distributions

Per-dimension quality distributions. Dashed line: default gate threshold τ=0.55.

Per-sample quality scores are released with every sample, enabling downstream users to filter by desired quality thresholds. The composite score weights identity preservation and perceptual quality highest (0.20 each), followed by alignment, boundary, and face integrity (0.15 each).
